Scrum Roles

RoleCore Responsibilities
Product Owner (PO) Owns the backlog and priorities. Ambassador for the product. Makes timely decisions about scope and features. Assesses value and prioritises stories for maximum business impact. Available to the team for requirements questions.
Scrum Master (SM) Removes impediments, coaches the team. Facilitates without coercing. Builds a trusting, safe environment where problems can be raised without blame. Helps the PO manage the backlog and interact with stakeholders. Establishes a culture of experimentation.
Dev Team / Squad Builds and tests the product. Collaborates to deliver the sprint commitment. Continuously improves. Transparent about work and challenges. Holds fellow members accountable to the team's Definition of Done.

As a Squad Member

Attend the Daily Standup. Raise blockers immediately. Start testing as soon as a story is developed — don't wait for the end of the sprint. The Dev Team (including you) decides HOW the work gets done.

Real Example — Credit Card Payment

ElementContent
User Story As a traveller, I want to pay for my ticket with a credit card, so that I can get my ticket immediately.
Acceptance Criteria Accept Visa & Mastercard only · Validate expiry & CVV · Show error if declined · OTP confirmation required · Show 'Payment Successful' screen
Definition of Done All AC tests pass (manual & automated) · No open P1/P2 defects · Code reviewed & merged · Tested on Chrome, Firefox, mobile · PO has accepted the story

Definition of Done

DoD Guidelines — All 7 Must Be Met
  1. Agreed between the team and the PO.
  2. Realistic (not aspirational).
  3. Within the control of the team — not dependent on someone outside the team.
  4. Updated regularly as team practices change or issues are identified.
  5. Short (5–8 items) — any longer and people will ignore it.
  6. Well understood by all; not ambiguous.
  7. Visible — put it on your task board.

Testing in Agile — 12-Step Process Map

Phase#Activity
PLAN1Involved upfront in Backlog Refinement
2Ensure user stories are clearly understood in Sprint Planning
3Flesh out AC, DoD, and provide estimates
DESIGN4Design test cases — both positive AND negative
5Automation engineers create test scripts (regression pack)
6Peer review test cases and scripts
EXECUTE7Story developed → start executing tests, raise defects
8Run regression test pack once manual tests pass
9Exploratory testing on high-risk / impacted areas
10UI & UX testing
EXTEND11Performance testing — are pages loading quickly?
12Test on different browsers, devices, operating systems

Practical Tips for Day 1

What to Ask and Do on Day 1
  • Find out the sprint cadence — when does it start/end? When is planning, standup, retro?
  • Know your team's roles — who is the PO? SM? Where do you go for requirements vs. blockers?
  • Ask to see the Product Backlog — get familiar with the system you're building before writing a single line of code or test.
  • Ask for the Definition of Done — if they don't have one, offer to help create one: instant value add.
  • Ask how defects are raised — Jira? Azure DevOps? Trello? Learn the tool fast.
  • Attend Backlog Refinement from Day 1 — don't wait to be invited. Testing starts here, not at end of sprint.
  • Your first standup: "Yesterday I did X. Today I'll do Y. I have [no] blockers." Keep it to 1 minute.
  • Speak up early — Scrum values courage and openness. If you don't understand something, ask — it helps the whole team.
